    Film Therapy
    Also known as "reel therapy" or "cinematherapy"

    📖 About Film Therapy

    Film therapy has shown potential in improving depression, anxiety, and other mental health issues. Through watching and reflecting on movies, people can unlock and experience repressed emotions — particularly effective for those experiencing emotional numbness.

    Participants can connect with characters or themes, gain new perspectives on personal issues, and see improvements in anxiety, stress, depression, life satisfaction, emotional expression, and self-reflection. It can also release serotonin, dopamine, and oxytocin.

    🎞️ The Process

    1. Make a personal goal you hope to achieve in film therapy.
    2. Choose a film clip that best fits your goal.
    3. Watch the clip, focusing on characters or themes you feel connected to.
    4. Reflect on the feelings that arose and how they relate to yourself or your goal.
    💡 If this process helps you self-reflect or gain a new perspective — great! Repeat as needed. If not, try a different clip or a more achievable goal.

    You can also practice art or writing therapy alongside film therapy by drawing or writing about feelings that arise.

    Music Therapy
    Discover how music can affect mood and create deep emotional connections

    📖 About Music Therapy

    Music therapy is highly impactful as a form of self-help. By listening to and reflecting on songs, people can achieve great overall improvements in mental wellness. Personal music taste also has a highly positive impact on outcomes, activating neurological reward signals.

    Participants can connect with lyrics or expressed emotions, be moved by melodies and harmonies, and see improvements in stress, emotional expression, mood, depression, and even cognitive and motor functions. It can release serotonin and dopamine while lowering cortisol.

    🎧 The Process

    1. Make a personal goal you hope to achieve in music therapy.
    2. Choose a genre and song that best fits your preference or goal.
    3. Listen to the song, focusing on any emotions or thoughts that arise.
    4. Reflect on the feelings you experienced and how they relate to your goal.
    💡 If you aren't moved by the song, try a different one — or pick your own song outside this site.

    You can also practice art or writing therapy alongside music therapy by drawing or writing about feelings that arise.

    Art Therapy
    Learn how the act of creating art can be used therapeutically

    📖 About Art Therapy

    Art therapy has proven to have positive impacts on a wide variety of aspects of mental health — from depression and anxiety to overall quality of life. By drawing or sketching about personal issues, people can reflect on themselves and see their problems in a different light.

    Participants can safely express their feelings without judgment, reflect on personal issues from a new angle, and see improvements in depression, anxiety, distress, mood, quality of life, coping, and self-esteem.

    🖌️ The Process

    1. Make a personal goal you hope to achieve in art therapy.
    2. Grab a pencil, paper, or whatever else you may use, and draw (no artistic skill required!).
    3. Focus on your goal or current feelings throughout the process.
    4. After finishing, reflect on your art and see how you chose to express your emotions in a new light.
    💡 If you struggle to find inspiration, simply sketch whatever comes to mind.

    You can also practice art therapy alongside film or music therapy — just draw feelings that arise from a clip or song.

    Writing Therapy
    Expressive writing as a tool for self-reflection and emotional processing

    📖 About Writing Therapy

    Writing therapy, sometimes called "expressive writing," is a method for people to disclose their feelings and personal events in a safe, comfortable way. By writing about personal topics, people can reflect on themselves and their experiences to better understand them.

    Participants can safely express themselves without judgment, immerse themselves in personal issues and events to gain a better understanding, and see improvements in emotional expression, self-reflection, PTSD, and even heart rate.

    📓 The Process

    1. Make a personal goal you hope to achieve in writing therapy.
    2. Begin writing, focusing on personal topics or events and immersing yourself in them.
    3. Consider your thoughts and emotions about your topic and try to incorporate them into your writing.
    4. When finished, reflect on your writing and any emotions that arose throughout the process.
    💡 Longer, more frequent sessions are ideal for maximum positive impact. Repeat as needed.

    You can also practice writing therapy alongside film or music therapy — just write about feelings that arise from a clip or song.
